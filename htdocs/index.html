Findings and Recommendations for Corn Belt Farmers

Learn the results of a five-year, USDA-NIFA research project, focused on farm management practices and their potential for making corn-based agriculture more resilient. The practices provide resiliency in times of drought, reducing soil and nutrient losses under saturated soil conditions, decreasing farm field nitrogen losses, retaining carbon in the soil, and ensuring crop and soil productivity.

February 11, from 1 p.m. to 3 p.m.

Climate & Ag Research by Next Generation Scientists Summarized in New Booklet

Learn about research being conducted, by our project's graduate students and post doctoral students, in a new booklet now available for viewing online.

Prepared for a poster symposium to be held in Washinton, D.C., October 15-16, the booklet contains 31 research summaries, describing the work of some of the next generation, climate and agriculture scientists, who are currently conducting their research with our project, under the supervision of our Principal Investigators and faculty at participating universities.

"Soil is Life" - New Video

 

 

 

Soil quality impacts water resources, biodiversity, food security and climate change. It is the engine of economic development, in relation to agricultural production. In this video presentation Rattan Lal, Principal Investigator with the Sustainable Corn Project and Ohio State University professor, shares key findings of his work with the Sustainable Corn Project and talks about why “soil is life and life is soil.”
